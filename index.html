Who is going to win the next superbowl?

It seems the Philadelphia Eagles and the New York Giants actually came out on top here, both winning all of their games so far.
Now that we've seen which teams are winning games, let's start to think about what factors drive positive outcomes at a more granular level.
We can break this down by thinking about games in the context of offensive and defensive performance.
One of the hallmark indicators of a positive offensive play is high quality ball handling. Did the team execute when needed? Or did they crack under pressure? Thus passing accuracy is a play-level metric that is imperative to consider when trying to distinguish between successful teams on offense and unsuccessful ones.
Next, applying a defensive perspective, arguably the most important consideration to make is whether tackles are being made when they need to be. This is the main tool to stop a strong offense, after all. Are players on a given team making their tackles? We can look at a team's tackle accuracy to examine this.
